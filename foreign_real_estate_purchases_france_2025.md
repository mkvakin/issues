# Foreign Real Estate Purchases in French Cities (2025)

**Compiled:** 2026-04-26
**Scope:** Residential property purchases by non-French buyers in metropolitan France, 2025 data (with 2022/2023/2024 comparisons where official 2025 numbers lag).
**Sources:** Notaires de France (Conseil supérieur du notariat), Notaires du Grand Paris, SeLoger, MonChasseurImmo, Côte d'Azur Sotheby's, Knight Frank, BNP Paribas Real Estate, Investropa, Connexion France, Boursorama, Patrimoine Privé, Properstar.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

- **National foreign-buyer share (most cited official figure):** ~2% of older-home transactions in 2025, by **non-resident** foreigners (Notaires de France definition: buyer lives abroad).
- **Broader share including foreign nationals living in France:** ~6% of all buyers nationally (SeLoger).
- **In Paris specifically:** non-residents reached **3.4% in 2025** (a 10-year high); foreign **residents** add another 9.6% (Île-de-France average), so ~13% of Paris-region buyers are non-French nationals.
- **Top non-resident nationality nationally:** **Belgians** (~20% of foreign purchases), having overtaken the British (17%, down from 35% in 2015) due to Brexit.
- **Top non-resident nationality in Paris:** **Americans** (23 to 25% of non-resident purchases), with the highest median budget (~€825,000).
- **PACA captures ~21% of all foreign purchases nationally**; Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur is France's foreign-buyer heartland.
- **Russian buyers, formerly dominant on the Riviera and in Courchevel, have effectively exited** the market post-2022. Americans, Gulf states, and Asian buyers are absorbing the void.

---

## 2. Critical Caveat: Three Different "Foreign Buyer" Populations

French datasets use three distinct definitions, and this is THE single biggest source of confusion when comparing numbers.

| Population | Who they are | National share (2024 to 2025) | Where measured |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Foreign non-residents** | Live abroad, buy a property in France (typically second home or investment) | **~2%** of older-home transactions | Notaires de France (official, notarized) |
| **Foreign residents** | Foreign nationals living and working in France (often long-settled immigrants) | **~9.6% in Île-de-France**; varies regionally | Notaires du Grand Paris |
| **All non-French buyers** | Both groups combined | **~5.9 to 6%** nationally | SeLoger / aggregator |

**Why this matters:**
- The "Belgians 20%, British 17%" ranking refers to **non-residents**, mostly second-home and luxury buyers.
- The "Chinese 15%, Portuguese 11%, Tunisian 8%" ranking refers to **residents** in Paris apartments, reflecting France's settled foreign-national population, not luxury holiday buyers.
- Mixing the two in one ranking gives a misleading picture. The report below keeps them separate.

### 4.1 PACA captures ~21% of ALL foreign purchases in France
Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur is by far the heaviest foreign-buyer region in France in absolute count. Within it, the Var département (Saint-Tropez, Hyères, Toulon) is dominant for Belgian and German buyers, while the Alpes-Maritimes (Nice, Cannes, Antibes, Monaco-adjacent) attracts a more diverse and higher-budget mix.

## 5. City-By-City Detail With Nationality Breakdown

### 5.1 Paris (Intramuros)
- **Non-resident foreign-buyer share:** **3.4% in 2025** (10-year high, +1.2pp YoY); 2.8% in 2022.
- **Foreign-resident share:** part of Île-de-France's 9.6% resident-foreigner buyer base; in Paris itself the resident-foreigner share is meaningfully higher.
- **Top non-resident nationalities (Notaires du Grand Paris 2022, with 2025 directional updates):**

| Rank | Nationality | Share of Paris non-resident purchases | Median budget |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **United States** | 23 to 25% (~200+ acquisitions/yr) | **€825,000** (€12,890/m², 64m² median) |
| 2 | **Lebanon** | 12% | €800,000+ |
| 3 | **Italy** | 9% | smaller apartments (92% buy apartments) |
| 4 | **Germany** | 8% | €400 to 600K |
| 5 | **Belgium** | 6% | mid-range |
| 6 | **United Kingdom** | declining (~5%) | €620K |
| - | Asia-Pacific (China, Korea, Japan) | rising fast | varies |

- **Most-targeted arrondissements:** **6th** (11% of sales to non-residents) and **7th** (13%). Also strong: 1st, 4th, 8th, 16th.
- **Median non-resident budget in Paris:** €635,100 for a 51m² apartment at €12,450/m².

|---|---|---|

| 3 | Tunisian | 8% |

For houses in Île-de-France: Portuguese 21%, Romanian 13%, Algerian 9%. Median price €314,000 (vs. €370,000 for French residents).

### 5.2 Nice (Alpes-Maritimes)
- **Foreign luxury-buyer share:** ~55% on the Côte d'Azur (was 40% post-lockdown).
- **Top nationalities:** Italians (geographic proximity, dominant), British, Belgians, Americans (rising), Germans, Russians (historically; now gone).
- **Profile:** Apartments ~€4,229/m²; houses ~€5,490/m². Average property ~€800,000 across the Riviera.

### 5.3 Cannes
- **Top nationalities:** Italians, Americans, British, Belgians, Middle Eastern (Saudis, Qataris, UAE), Northern Europeans.
- **Profile:** Apartments €4,424 to €7,115/m². Festival-driven prestige; trophy penthouses. Strong post-2022 American surge thanks to dollar strength.

### 5.4 Antibes / Cap d'Antibes / Saint-Jean-Cap-Ferrat
- **Top nationalities:** Americans, British, Belgians, Italians, Middle Eastern (UAE, Saudi).
- **Profile:** Ultra-prime peninsula villas; deepest international mix on the Riviera.

### 5.5 Saint-Tropez
- **Foreign-buyer share (luxury):** **~55% of the prime market**, up from 40% post-lockdown.
- **Top nationalities:**
  - **Americans (resurgent)**: dominant since 2022, replacing Russian flow
  - Belgians, British, Italians
  - Middle Eastern (Saudis, UAE, Qatar)
  - Northern European (Belgians, Dutch, Scandinavians)
- **Profile:** Heart of the market €15M to €30M. Average days-on-market 45 in 2025. New-build median price/m² up 29% over 5 years.

### 5.6 Monaco-Adjacent (Beaulieu, Cap-Ferrat, Roquebrune-Cap-Martin)
- **Top nationalities:** Italians, Russians (formerly), British, Belgians, Middle Eastern, increasingly Americans.

### 5.7 Bordeaux
- **Top nationalities:** British, Belgians, Germans (wine investors), Americans (rising), Dutch.
- **Profile:** Vineyard properties €350,000+. High-speed-train link to Paris driving second-home appeal. Bordeaux remains a "value alternative" to Paris luxury.

### 5.8 Lyon
- **Top nationalities:** Swiss (proximity), Americans, British, Belgians.
- **Profile:** ~4.5% rental yield. Less foreign-skewed than Paris, but rising urban-investment story.

### 5.9 Marseille and Aix-en-Provence
- **Top nationalities:** Belgians, British, Italians, Algerians (resident), Moroccans (resident), Americans (Aix luxury).
- **Profile:** Aix-en-Provence is a strong American-buyer micro-market alongside the Luberon.

### 5.10 Toulouse and Montpellier (Occitanie)
- **Top nationalities:** Belgians, British, Spanish (proximity), Dutch.
- **Profile:** Lower entry prices than PACA; Occitanie captures ~18% of national foreign purchases.

### 5.11 Nantes / Brittany Coast (Saint-Malo, Dinard, Morbihan)
- **Top nationalities:** British (dominant rural), Dutch, Belgians, Germans.
- **Profile:** Rural houses; budget-friendly; 5% rental yield in Nantes urban area.

## 6. Key Region Detail

### 6.1 French Riviera / Côte d'Azur (PACA's Alpes-Maritimes + Var coast)
- **Status:** France's #1 foreign-buyer destination.
- **Foreign luxury-segment share:** ~55% in 2025 (40% in 2021).
- **National-level data:** PACA captures 21% of all foreign purchases in France; non-resident share 3.8% (peaked at 5.2% in 2015).
- **Nationality mix:** Italians + Belgians + British + Americans + Middle Eastern, with Russians having exited.
- **Sotheby's 2025 Riviera report:** Italians, Americans, and Germans flagged as the three most active nationalities.

### 6.2 Provence (inland: Luberon, Aix-en-Provence, Vaucluse, Alpilles)
- **Top nationalities:** Americans (Luberon, Aix), British, Belgians, Germans, Dutch.
- **Profile:** Stone mas farmhouses, bastides, Provençal villas. Multi-million-euro luxury segment driven by Anglosphere buyers.

### 6.3 Dordogne / Lot-et-Garonne / Périgord
- **Status:** Historic British buyer heartland ("Dordogneshire").
- **Top nationalities:** British (overwhelming dominance, despite post-Brexit decline), Dutch, Belgians.
- **Profile:** £500,000 second-home average for British. 22.4% rise in property activity reflects ongoing rural-French appeal.

### 6.4 French Alps (Chamonix, Megève, Courchevel, Méribel, Val d'Isère, Val Thorens, Tignes)
- **Top nationalities:**
  - **British**: long dominant in Chamonix, Megève, the "British corridor" Northern Alps; some retreat post-Brexit but still major.
  - **Belgians**: significant presence in Courchevel, Méribel, Val d'Isère.
  - **Americans**: surging in Courchevel, Val d'Isère, Megève; dollar strength driver.
  - **Russians**: historically dominant in Courchevel, gone since 2022.
  - **Middle Eastern (UAE, Saudi, Qatar)**: trophy chalets, growing.
  - **Asian (Chinese, Singaporean, Hong Kong)**: emerging luxury cohort.
  - **Swiss**: Annecy, Haute-Savoie, border resorts.
- **Profile:** Trophy ski-in/ski-out chalets in Courchevel 1850 reach €10M+. Energy rating (DPE) and parking now decisive in price.

### 6.5 Brittany (Bretagne)
- **Top nationalities:** British (rural houses), Dutch, Belgians, Germans.
- **Profile:** ~50% of British, Belgian, Dutch foreign purchases nationally are in rural France, much of it Brittany.

### 6.6 Loire Valley
- **Top nationalities:** British, Belgians, Dutch, Americans (chateaux niche).
- **Profile:** Chateaux, manoirs; strong Anglosphere identity.

### 6.7 Alsace / Grand Est
- **Top nationalities:** **Germans** (proximity-driven dominant), Swiss.
- **Profile:** Cross-border buyers with cultural and language ties.

### 6.8 Haute-Savoie / Savoie (Lakes Annecy and Léman)
- **Top nationalities:** Swiss (working in Geneva, residing in France), British, Dutch (12% of regional non-residents), Belgians.
- **Profile:** "Frontalier" market driven by Geneva commuters.

### 6.9 Creuse / Limousin (rural central France)
- **Top nationalities:** Dutch (8% of regional non-residents), British, Belgians.
- **Profile:** Cheapest region in France (~€1,200/m²); attracts retirees and small-budget rural buyers.

## 8. Key Trends (2022 to 2025)

1. **Belgian-British rotation is complete:** Belgians overtook British as #1 non-resident nationality in 2022 to 2023; the rotation is now structural, not a blip.
2. **Americans are the fastest-growing high-end cohort.** Paris, the Riviera, the Alps, the Luberon: dollar strength + remote work has made this the standout 2022 to 2025 trend in French luxury.
3. **Russian exit is a permanent feature.** Sanctions, frozen assets, visa restrictions. Replaced unevenly: Americans on the Riviera, Gulf states in trophy luxury, Asian buyers in branded developments.
4. **Paris non-resident share at a 10-year high:** 3.4% in 2025, with the 6th and 7th arrondissements over-represented.
5. **PACA still rules absolute volume** with 21% of all foreign purchases despite share losses since 2015.
6. **Dutch are the rising mid-market story** with +45% inquiry growth, focused on Creuse, Brittany, Haute-Savoie.
7. **Asian buyers (Chinese, Korean, Japanese, Singaporean, Hong Kong)** are the emerging luxury cohort, particularly in Paris and the Alps.
8. **Brexit drag on British buyers is real but cushioned** by structural attachment to Dordogne, Brittany, the Alps.

## 9. Comparison: France vs. Italy (2025)

| Metric | France | Italy |
|---|---|---|
| Foreign non-resident share of older-home transactions | **~2%** | n/a (Italy doesn't separate cleanly) |
| All-foreign share of residential transactions | ~6% (resident + non-resident) | **~14%** |
| #1 foreign nationality by transaction count | Belgians (~20%) | Germans (~70%) |
| Top urban-luxury nationality | Americans (Paris 23%) | Americans (Milan 26.6%) |
| Top regional foreign-buyer hub | PACA (21% of all foreign purchases) | Puglia (2,300 of 8,600 foreign purchases 2020 to 2025) |
| Russian exit absorbed by | Americans, Gulf states, Asian buyers | Americans, Gulf states |
| Foreign luxury-segment share | ~55% on Riviera | 50 to 65% nationally |
| Most striking 2022 to 2025 trend | American surge (dollar + remote work) | American surge in cities; Sicily/Sardinia/Puglia rise in regions |

**Key structural difference:** France's foreign buyer market is much smaller in share (6% vs. 14%) but more diverse in nationality. Italy's market is dominated by Germans buying second homes; France's market is split among Belgians, British, Americans, Germans, Dutch, with each holding distinct regional turf.

## 10. Methodological Notes And Open Questions

- **Resident vs. non-resident is the single most confusing axis** in French data. Always check which population a number describes.
- **2025 numbers are partial.** Notaires de France full 2025 study publishes in mid-2026; figures here use the 2023 full study, 2025 directional updates, and Q1 to Q3 2025 datapoints.
- **City-level nationality breakdowns for Lyon, Marseille, Toulouse, Bordeaux, Nantes, Strasbourg are sparser than for Paris and the Riviera.** The Notaires du Grand Paris publishes Paris/Île-de-France detail; other cities rely on agency data which is less rigorous.
- **Open questions worth deeper research if needed:**
  - 2025 full-year nationality mix (publishing 2026).
  - Cash vs. mortgage split by nationality.
  - Visa-driven flows (Talent Passport, "Passeport Talent", investor routes).
  - Capital from sanctioned-Russian-replacement buyers: precise composition.
