## Strategic shopping plan

Given Silicon Valley driving distances, a sane two-stop weekly run beats three stops.

### Primary stop: Trader Joe's (the workhorse, ~70% of the list)
- All dairy, eggs, butter, cheese (havarti, swiss, parmesan)
- Bananas, apples (bagged), packaged salads
- Pasta, pasta sauce, apple juice
- Pastrami / roast beef
- Ground beef (grass-fed)
- Mini watermelon, conventional strawberries

### Secondary stop: Sprouts (for what TJ's can't do well)
- Flavored kefir (strawberry/peach), TJ's only carries plain
- Fresh berries when the weekly ad shows a sale (very frequent: $1.99–2.99 strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)
- Organic eggs ($5.29 beats TJ's)
- Tomatoes, cucumbers, pears (better produce variety and weekly produce sales)
- Specialty items like grass-fed meat value packs

### Skip Safeway as a routine stop unless:
- You have a Safeway "Just for U" account with personalized digital coupons; loyalty pricing can occasionally beat Sprouts
- You need pharmacy, household goods, or a one-stop trip
- A specific weekly ad item is dramatically cheaper (e.g. watermelons at $0.39/lb in summer, or 4-for-$5 berry deals)

## Things to be aware of

- **Sprouts weekly ad runs Wednesday–Tuesday.** Check `sprouts.com/weekly-ad` Tuesday night before shopping. Their produce sales are real and often beat TJ's.
- **Trader Joe's has no sales, no coupons, no loyalty program.** What you see is what you pay, every week.
- **Bay Area Safeway is the most expensive of the three at full price** but the most discount-able with Just for U. Without coupons, do not bother.
- **Healthy-eating note:** Sprouts has the strongest organic and grass-fed offering by SKU count. If "as organic as possible" is the priority and budget allows, Sprouts deserves more of the list. If "healthy at the lowest price" is the priority, TJ's wins by a wide margin.
