# Marido: Statements From the 2007 + 2009 Evaluations

> **Purpose**: This document isolates everything the two evaluations say *about Marido* (Her first US husband, an Air Force officer). It exists because the prior integration of these evaluations into [character_analysis.md](../character_analysis.md) conflated some Marido material with her material (specifically: the explosive alcohol-using stepfather is *Marido's*, not her). Separating cleanly here so the she analysis can be re-run without that contamination.

> **Scope**: Factual extraction only. No interpretation, no diagnosis, no analysis. Attribution-tagged where the source is a collateral or a particular evaluator. Where the source agent's text was OCR-garbled or ambiguous, that is flagged.

> - **Wilbourn (2007)**: Parenting Time Evaluation, El Paso County Colorado, case 06DR1817, by Mike Wilbourn, Psy.D. Battery on Marido: MMPI-2, MCMI-III, 16PF Fifth Edition, mental status exam, home visit, collaterals.

## 5. Test Results (Wilbourn 2007)

### MMPI-2 (verbatim)
- "Unrealistic claims of personal virtue as reflected in this profile, are common in custody cases where admitting faults might be perceived as detrimental to the case. The resulting MMPI-2 profile is not likely to yield much useful information about Marido because he was too guarded to cooperate with the self-appraisal and, as a result, this profile may underestimate his psychological maladjustment. Test interpretation should proceed with caution as a result."
- "Individuals with this profile tend to be passive, dependent and they readily form deep emotional attachments but are overly sensitive to rejection. This client is likely to have a problem with a lack of assertiveness and seems to have difficulty dealing effectively with interpersonal conflict. He seems to require an excessive amount of emotional support from his spouse."
- "He reported a number of physical complaints and symptoms of severe anxiety and depressed mood that should be taken into consideration in a diagnostic formulation."

### MCMI-III (verbatim)
- "The MCMI-III profile of this man suggests that he is fearfully dependent, socially anxious and self-demeaning and dejected. Hesitant about asserting himself, he may lean on others for guidance and security. He may strive to assume a passive and self-sacrificing role in many relationships. Notable may be his lack of initiative, his avoidance of adult forms of autonomy and his willingness to accept a deserved blame and criticism. Unusually dependent and insecure he may feel vulnerable if he does not receive support from those who usually provide it. Despite his dependency and his inclination that others are trying to exploit him, he is likely to resent those on whom he must lean because they are often abusive and demanding. He occasionally expresses anger towards them. He typically discharges his anger in an indirect manner; however, his insecurity is threatened by any expression of resentment. Fearful of rebuff from others, he may withdraw from he experiences as painful social relationships to prevent himself from expressing resentment."
- "This man reported feelings of apathy and weakness and his tendency to succumb easily to physical exhaustion and illness may reflect a persistent and chronic dysthymia or major depression. Simple responsibilities may demand more energy than he can muster and he may describe his life as empty."
- "He is also insecure and prone to self-pity and self-doubt. A worrier who has an overly perfectionist attitude, he feels he cannot perform his expectations or his goals. He sets too unrealistic goals for himself and ends up feeling despondent and dejected when he can't reach them. He believes he should suppress and reject any current emotions and is markedly susceptible to anxiety and depression."

### 16PF Fifth Edition (verbatim)
- "The infrequency index was within expected limits, indicating that Marido read the items carefully and understood what was required. Apparently he did not present a particularly positive or negative impression of himself, therefore the information in this report is likely to be accurate."
- "In addition to his social withdrawal, Marido exhibits slightly below-average ego strength affecting his ability to defer his needs when appropriate and to modulate the expression of his personality as the occasion demands... His global emotional adjustment is above-average but the mediocrity of his ego strength raises questions about how reliable that adjustment really is. The pattern of traits suggests that his current positive adaptation may be the result of fortuitous circumstances rather than psychological strength."
- "He has a tendency to deny problems with tension or anxiety a finding that must be examined in the context of the testing circumstances. It may be that his life at present is particularly free of stress. However, it must be considered whether he is refusing to acknowledge even the possibility of becoming anxious."
- "Even though his level of conventionality is slightly below-average, this by itself probably represents an adequate degree of concern with living up to the standards of others and following the rules. Gaining the good opinion of his peers is probably a reasonable motivating force for him. He is a cheerful, lively, spontaneous person who generally has enough energy to fulfill his agenda. People will sometimes think he is too excitable."

### Wilbourn 2007 Diagnostic Impression (verbatim)
- "Diagnostically, he best appears to fit on Axis I Dysthymic Disorder and on Axis II Dependent Personality Traits, Avoidant Personality Traits, Depressive Personality Features, and Passive Aggressive Personality Features."
- "The major personality features described previously reflect long-term or chronic states that don't likely to manifest themselves in relatively transient, waxing and waning in their prominence and depending on the presence of environmental stressors."

### Wilbourn 2007 Mental Status (verbatim)

He became slightly emotional when talking about some areas of his situation, especially where he reflected back on what he thought was a happy history, which later turned out to be a disappointment."

## 6. Test Results (Gutierrez 2009)

### PSI (Parenting Stress Inventory)
- Scale elevations indicate that in Marido's view parenting the child is significantly more difficult for him than for 95% of other parents due to the child's inability to switch either emotionally or socially from one social setting to another; she is easily emotionally upset, she overreacts to changes, avoids strangers, and finds it difficult to calm down once upset.
- "There are also indications that in spite of these difficulties, there is a growing positive relationship. The fragility of the father/child relationship merits remedial efforts, and will require time and therapeutic intervention."
- "Significant potential that Marido may feel rejected by the child."
- May produce "symptoms consistent with depression"

### PAM (Parenting Alliance Measure)
- "Marido views his co-parenting relationship with her as 'dysfunctional'. He considers she to be a poor parent and feels that the parents do not view the child or her needs similarly."

### Gutierrez 2009 Clinical Synthesis (verbatim, key passages)
- "Marido was open and cooperative with the evaluation process, and offered to make extra trips to the Bay Area in order to accommodate the appointment scheduling. He was well oriented to time and place, and appeared emotionally and interpersonally appropriate."
- "Marido is a bright and intense young man who speaks articulately and thoughtfully. Even when discussing difficult or painful things he seemed unusually candid."
- "Marido is similar to most others who desire closeness with others, and who challenge themselves to live within a moral standard that he and others consider reasonable. Marido seems to find it difficult to assert himself, and especially to express negative emotions. Similar to her, Marido has strong dependency needs, and desires to feel close to others, seeking their approval through his conforming behavior. He may have somewhat limited insight into the motivations of others' behaviors, and can appear naive. As a result of his being disappointed in the past he may appear suspicious of others at this time. **However, dissimilar to her, he does not seem to be manipulative.**"
- "His decision to fight for time with his child appears to cost him a great deal emotionally, and may lead to symptoms consistent with depression."
- "Marido is willing to expend energy building relationships with others, but because he finds it difficult to express negative emotions as it occurs, it builds up and may overwhelm him. He is encouraged to address ways to better process his emotional experiences through individual therapy. When he knows he will find himself in difficult situations (such as co-parenting exchanges), he is encouraged to map out potential positive behavioral scenarios in order to envision and magnify positive outcomes for the child."
- "As a parent, it is this evaluator's impression that Marido has many positive qualities. He is warm and responsive to the child, and able to focus on her needs. He thinks ahead toward that which may facilitate her comfort and is able and willing to provide physically affectionate and emotionally regulatory interactions. Most importantly, he encourages her to experience her own competency and to challenge herself in multiple settings, and he provides her with praise and feedback for doing so. Additionally, Marido seems willing to avail himself of resources for parenting guidance, and indicated he is willing to take parenting classes, and to participate in co-parenting counseling."

## 12. Caveats For Re-Running the Analysis

1. **Source-text quality**: The PDFs are scanned images with no text layer. Both extractions came from page-by-page image OCR via subagent reading. Some passages were OCR-garbled (notably the section on her stepfather, where the prior conflation entered). For any claim that is going to be load-bearing in a diagnosis, verify against the original PDF page.

2. **Both evaluators tested both parents**. The Wilbourn and Gutierrez findings *about her* belong in the she analysis; the Wilbourn and Gutierrez findings *about Marido* are in this document and should not be migrated back into the she analysis except where they bear on the partner-selection signature (Section 4.5 of [character_analysis.md](../character_analysis.md)).

3. **The "explosive alcohol-using stepfather" detail belongs to Marido, not she**. The prior version of [character_analysis.md](../character_analysis.md) Section 1.6(e) attributed this detail to her family of origin in error. That section needs revision before being used to drive any clinical conclusion about her developmental etiology.

4. **What the evaluations actually say about her family of origin** (cleanly): biological father left when she was age 3-4 ("dad just left; he didn't want to stay; he came to see me, but I can't remember much"); culturally normalized in her telling ("it wasn't that uncommon that dads would just take off, and kids would grow up with just their moms"); mother stayed home with her until age 3-4; mother described in collateral as "a wonderful woman"; mother visited her in the US repeatedly across the marriage and provided childcare during Anastasia's first year. **No alcohol-using or explosive stepfather is documented for her in either evaluation, on a careful reading.** Her own stepfather, if any, is not characterized in the source extracts as explosive or alcohol-using.

5. **What the evaluations say about her *own* alcohol/substance use**: Per Marido, "She says she used to drink alcohol but has never had more than a glass of wine with dinner since I have known her. I have never known her to abuse any other substances." She herself denies substance abuse. No collateral contradicts this.
