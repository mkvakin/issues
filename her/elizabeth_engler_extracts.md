> **Purpose**: This document isolates everything the two evaluations say *about her*. It is the companion to [jason_engler_extracts.md](jason_engler_extracts.md), which isolates the Marido material. Together these two documents cleanly separate the two parents' material from the two evaluations, so the she analysis in [character_analysis.md](../character_analysis.md) can be re-run without conflation.

> **Scope**: Factual extraction only. No interpretation, no diagnosis, no analysis. Attribution-tagged where the source is a collateral or a particular evaluator. Where the source agent's text was OCR-garbled or ambiguous, that is flagged.

> - **Wilbourn (2007)**: Parenting Time Evaluation, El Paso County Colorado, case 06DR1817, by Mike Wilbourn, Psy.D. Battery on her: MMPI-2, MCMI-III, 16PF Fifth Edition, mental status exam, home visit (San Ramon CA), collaterals.

## 7. Test Results (Gutierrez 2009)

### PSI (Parenting Stress Inventory)
- "Scale elevations are clinically significant in the area of the spousal relationship. There seems to be an indication of conflict in the area of the spousal relationship... her relationship with the child closely while not paying attention to herself as a successful parent. Additionally, a high score in the area of the child's moodiness may indicate impairment in the attachment between mother and child. This may be a result of attachment ruptures caused by the current timeshare, or it is possible that the moodiness reflects underlying attachment difficulties in the mother/child relationship."

### PAM (Parenting Alliance Measure)
- "She views her co-parenting relationship with Marido as 'problematic,' which is not quite as negative as Marido's view. The difference appears to be that she feels Marido considers her to be an adequate parent to the child. She, like Marido, feels that the parents view the child and her needs dis-similarly."

### Gutierrez 2009 Clinical Assessment Summary (verbatim, key passages)
- "She was cooperative with the evaluation process, and was very well oriented. There were several points during the evaluation process when her demeanor appeared unpredictably hostile and aggressive, and seemed to expect that the rules and boundaries established for others should not apply to her."
- "She is a bright and attractive woman, who described her experiences growing up in glowing terms. She was somewhat vague and dismissive when discussing her emotional responses to events in her life, and tended to emphasize and embellish her virtues."
- "Based on the assessment and interview data, it is this evaluator's opinion that she has many strengths to offer as a parent. She is bright, able to focus on the child, and encourages the child to read and experience cultural and educational activities such as going to the aquarium, playing with friends and their children, bookstores, museums, etc. Psychologically, she is able to utilize rationality and foresight, and does not appear to be prone to loss of emotional control. The data indicate she has limited psychological insight, and she can be manipulative and intrusive in her interactions with others. Additionally, she appears to shut down or withdraw emotionally in some situations, appearing unpredictable. At other times she seems to consider herself outside of the rules which apply to others (such as knocking on a door which says please do not knock). The child's emotionally dysregulated responses while in her care may confuse she; she appears to not know how to respond when the child threatens to cry and scream, suggesting that she has no or limited bases upon which to model her parenting."
- "The data further suggest that she tends to focus on and emphasize her own positive qualities, overlooking the impact of her own behavior on others, and minimizing the value of others. Additionally, the historical data suggests that she has been adventurous and quite capable of making decisions on her own prior to marriage. Leaving the Bay Area, leaving her job, getting married and having a child, and getting divorced are all decisions she describes as either too fast or decisions and events that somehow she did not fully endorse. Her presentation of herself in these past life changing events as passive seems somewhat incongruous."
- "However, this underlying passivity may manifest in her parenting. Namely, there is concern that she may grant the child too much decision making power, and yet unconsciously requires from the child an unhealthy focus on her (she) as the only adult who can meet the child's needs. Her own needs may, in the parenting relationship, tend to overshadow the child's developmental needs to go underground and therefore unmet."
- "One such current need, in this evaluator's opinion, is the child's need for exposure to structured social settings outside her family relationship with her mother."

### Gutierrez 2009 forensic findings on the mother-child relationship (verbatim)
- "In the opinion of this evaluator the child is at risk for serious long-term deleterious social and emotional relationships as a result of what appears to be a distorted and unhealthy relationship with her mother."
- "Although she genuinely believes she provides an emotionally stable and loving environment for the child, collateral information and direct observation indicate that the child's psychological well-being and social functioning are undermined by her parenting. As a result of this particular mother-daughter relationship, there is a risk that the child will continue to develop and sustain a suspiciousness and interpersonal hostility toward others which is likely to cause some harm to the child's ability to establish and sustain peer-relationships as well as function in the school setting."
- "While with her mother, the child's personality functioning seems unsociable and fearful; while with her father, the child's personality functioning seems age-appropriate, confident, resourceful and socially engaging."
- "This evaluator's concern is that the child's attachment relationship with her mother has developed in such a way as to address her mother's emotional needs rather than her own developmental needs. The child's regressive clinginess is encouraged by her mother who (perhaps unconsciously) may seek the emotional reward of the child's dependence on her."
- "The child demonstrates an anxious attachment to her mother, which may in the future cause her to forgo developmentally appropriate efforts to mature."
- "Her interpersonal style can be merging, manipulative and patronizing, creating mistrust and confusion in others (adults as well as the child)."

### Gutierrez 2009 (no formal Axis-coding)
- Gutierrez did not formally code Axis I/II diagnoses for her, but her clinical descriptions are consistent with Wilbourn's 2007 HPD-with-Narcissistic-and-Excessive-Compulsive-Traits picture.

## 14. Caveats For Re-Running the Analysis

1. **Source-text quality**: The PDFs are scanned images. Both extractions came from page-by-page image OCR via subagent reading. Some passages were OCR-garbled. For any claim that is going to be load-bearing in a diagnosis, verify against the original PDF page.

2. **She had no stepfather**: Her mother did not remarry after the biological father left. She was raised by her mother alone from age 3-4 onward. The "explosive alcohol-using stepfather" that appeared in the original character analysis is **Marido's** stepfather, not her; that material is correctly attributed in [jason_engler_extracts.md](jason_engler_extracts.md) Section 2. Any developmental etiology in [character_analysis.md](../character_analysis.md) that hangs on her having had a stepfather should be removed.

3. **What is documented about her developmental etiology**:
   - Father left around age 3-4; visited occasionally; alive in Russia at the time of evaluation
   - Mother stayed home with her until age 3-4; described as "wonderful," "very involved," structured Russian-traditional childhood with cultural enrichment; remained the dominant adult attachment in her life into adulthood; visited the US repeatedly; provided childcare for Anastasia
   - Only child; no half-siblings; maternal-side extended family includes a maternal uncle and his son (Her cousin) in Russia
   - Cultural belief explicit: "in Russia men are not worth much in the family"
   - Self-description of childhood as "blissful" and "great"
   - No documented abuse, neglect, or trauma in either evaluation
   - No documented psychiatric history, hospitalizations, or substance use prior to the marriage

4. **What the evaluations document about her in adulthood**:
   - Bright; engineering Master's; able to function professionally
   - Highly defensive on personality testing; low insight; image-managing
   - Able to use rationality and foresight; not prone to loss of emotional control (per Gutierrez 2009)
   - "Exceptional ego strength" and "firm sense of self" (per 16PF 2007)
   - "Above-average abstract reasoning" (per MCMI-III 2007)
   - HPD with Excessive-Compulsive and Narcissistic traits (Wilbourn Axis II 2007); no Axis I
   - Manipulative, intrusive, capable of parental alienation, considers herself outside the rules (Gutierrez 2009)
   - Anxious-preoccupied attachment to her daughter; enmeshed and exclusionary mother-child dynamic
   - Russian cultural framing of male devaluation
   - Functional somatic symptoms (gastrointestinal, headaches)
   - No paranoid ideation, hallucinations, or delusions documented in either MSE
   - No documented violence in 2007-2009; physical altercations alleged on both sides during the marriage but characterized by Gutierrez as "situational and mutual volatility" if real
   - No documented criminal acts in 2007-2009 (the most extreme behaviors in [character_analysis.md](../character_analysis.md), such as the tear gas attack and illegal cameras, are post-2009)
