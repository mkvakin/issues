# Foreign Real Estate Purchases in Italian Cities (2025)

**Compiled:** 2026-04-26
**Scope:** Residential property purchases by non-Italian buyers, 2025 data (with 2024 and 2023 comparisons where available).
**Sources:** Scenari Immobiliari, Tecnocasa, Gate-Away, Idealista, Engel & Völkers, Knight Frank, Cushman & Wakefield, Milano Finanza, Roma Today, Milano Today, Mi-Tomorrow.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

- **National foreign-buyer share (residential):** ~14% of all home purchases in 2025, up from 9.5% in 2019 and 12% in 2024.
- **Volume:** ~8,700 foreign families purchased homes in Italy in 2025 (vs. 8,000 pre-pandemic, 1,700 at the 2020 trough).
- **Spending:** €5.5 billion in 2025 (+10% YoY, +48% vs. pre-pandemic). Average €632,000 per buyer (Scenari Immobiliari).
- **Luxury segment:** Foreign investment up ~44% YoY. International clients now ~50 to 65% of prime/luxury transactions and ~58% of commercial volumes.
- **Dominant nationality (overall residential):** Germans, by a wide margin in pure transaction count (driven by second-home demand on the coast and lakes).
- **Dominant nationality in big-city/urban luxury:** Americans, particularly in Milan and Florence.
- **Geographic shift:** Tuscany, Umbria, Lazio losing share to Sicily, Sardinia, Puglia.

---

## 2. Critical Caveat: Different Datasets Tell Different Stories

The "share of foreign buyers" varies dramatically by source because each measures something different. Treat the numbers below with this in mind.

| Dataset | What it measures | Skew |
|---|---|---|
| **Scenari Immobiliari / Notarial data** | Closed transactions by foreign families | Heavy second-home/coastal skew, Germans dominate |
| **Tecnocasa / Idealista clicks** | Search interest and inquiries | Germans top inquiries, but does not equal closings |
| **Gate-Away (international portal)** | Inquiries from buyers already abroad | Anglosphere over-represented; budgets often <€100K |
| **Engel & Völkers / Knight Frank** | Luxury and prime segment only | Americans, UAE, North Europeans over-represented |
| **City-level realtor data (Milan)** | Specific market share within one city | Reflects urban investor profile, not vacation buyers |

**The practical synthesis:**
- If you want one number for Italy as a whole: ~14% of homes bought by non-Italians, with Germans as the single largest nationality (~70% of foreign-family purchases per Scenari Immobiliari).
- If you want the urban / luxury picture: Americans lead, then British, Germans, French, Northern Europeans.
- The Scenari "70% German" figure is real but reflects that Italy's foreign-buyer market is overwhelmingly driven by Northern-European second-home demand on the Adriatic, the Lakes, Trentino, Liguria, and Tuscany.

## 6. City-By-City Detail With Nationality Breakdown

### 6.1 Milan
- **Foreign share:** ~14% of residential purchases in 2025 (vs. 9.5% in 2019).
- **Top nationalities (Mi-Tomorrow / city data):**
  - **United States: 26.56%** of foreign buyers
  - Sweden: 8.33%
  - United Kingdom: 7.29%
  - Germany: 5.73%
  - Switzerland: 2.6%
  - Plus growing presence of French, Dutch, UAE, Asian buyers in central districts.
- **Profile:** 50.34% of foreign prospects budget over €1M. Average requested property value €1,781,924 (+28.37% vs. 2023).
- **Why Americans top Milan:** post-pandemic remote-work flexibility, dollar strength, perceived lifestyle premium vs. New York / SF / Miami pricing.

### 6.2 Rome
- **Foreign share:** ~9.54% in 2025, up from 7.48% in 2023.
- **Top nationalities (Roma Today / Gate-Away):**
  - United States (largest source)
  - United Kingdom
  - France
  - Plus Dutch, German, Belgian secondary
- **Profile:** Average requested property €1,149,020. Demand concentrated in historic center, Prati, Parioli, Pinciano.
- **Note:** Engel & Völkers reports Rome's premium transactions remain ~85% Italian-buyer, with foreign demand softening ~18.5% in early 2025 vs. peak before recovering. Foreign demand is concentrated in trophy assets, not the broader market.

### 6.3 Florence
- **Foreign share (luxury segment):** ~65% of prime buyers are non-Italian.
- **Top nationalities:**
  - **Germany: 24.4%** (city leader)
  - France: 12.4%
  - United States (very strong, particularly art-history and lifestyle buyers)
  - United Kingdom
  - Netherlands, Belgium
- **Profile:** Average luxury budget €1 to 1.5M; top prices reach €10,000/sqm in central micro-locations.
- **Why:** Cultural prestige, walkable historic core, strict urban-preservation laws limiting supply.

### 6.4 Venice
- **Foreign share:** Strong international presence; ~5.6% of all foreign luxury searches in Italy direct here.
- **Top nationalities:**
  - Germans (Venice is the standout German city target)
  - Americans
  - British
  - French
- **Profile:** ~40% of foreign purchases for second-home use, ~40% as investment (rentals). Supply is fixed; demand drives prices.

### 6.5 Trieste
- **Foreign share:** Historically among the highest in Italy; slight cooling in 2025.
- **Top nationalities:** Austrians, Germans, Slovenians (geographic proximity), Northern Europeans.
- **Profile:** Lower entry prices than Milan/Rome; expat-friendly, German-English commonly spoken.

### 6.6 Lake Como
- **Foreign share:** Foreign demand predominates in lakeside high-end segment. ~5% of all foreign luxury searches in Italy.
- **Top nationalities:**
  - **United States** (top, especially since 2022)
  - Germany
  - **Gulf countries (UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar)**
  - United Kingdom
  - Switzerland
- **Profile:** High-end property prices rose ~20% in 2023; sustained demand 2024 to 2025. Trophy lakeside villas dominate.

### 6.7 Tuscany (Florence excluded; rural and smaller cities)
- **Regional share of foreign-family purchases:** Dropped from 22% (2015) to 13% (2025). Still a major destination, just less dominant than a decade ago.
- **Top nationalities:**
  - Germans (largest in transactions)
  - Americans (largest in inquiry value, particularly Lucca, Chianti, Val d'Orcia)
  - British (Lucca area especially)
  - Dutch, French, Swiss
- **Hotspots:** Lucca, Chianti, Siena, Cortona, Val d'Orcia, Lunigiana (budget), Maremma.
- **Profile:** Knight Frank Wealth Report 2025 confirms Tuscany still the #1 prime second-home region for global UHNW buyers despite share losses.

### 6.8 Puglia
- **Regional share of foreign-family purchases:** Stable at ~17%. Leading region for foreign-family closings (2,300 of 8,600 foreign-family purchases 2020 to 2025).
- **Top nationalities:**
  - British
  - Dutch
  - Americans
  - Germans
  - French
- **Hotspots:** Ostuni (#1 in Italy), Carovigno, Cisternino, Lecce, Polignano a Mare, Monopoli, the Itria Valley.
- **Why:** Trulli architecture, lower entry prices, rapid tourism growth, English-speaking expat community.

### 6.9 Sicily
- **Regional share of foreign-family purchases:** Doubled from 9% (2015) to 18% (2025). Fastest-growing region.
- **Top nationalities:**
  - **British (largest single group)**, drawn to Caltagirone and the southeast
  - **French**, concentrated in Siracusa and Noto
  - Germans
  - Americans (rising)
- **Hotspots:** Noto, Siracusa, Caltagirone, Modica, Ragusa, Palermo, Taormina, the Aeolian Islands.
- **Why:** "1-euro home" media, lower entry prices, Mediterranean climate, baroque heritage.

### 6.10 Sardinia
- **Regional share of foreign-family purchases:** Surged from 1% (2015) to 9% (2025).
- **Top nationalities (luxury, Costa Smeralda):**
  - ~80% of luxury buyers in Costa Smeralda are foreign.
  - Russians historically dominant, now replaced by Americans, Gulf states (UAE, Saudi, Qatar), Germans, Swiss, British.
- **Hotspots:** Costa Smeralda, Porto Cervo, Olbia, Alghero, southern coast.

### 6.11 Lazio (excl. Rome)
- **Regional share:** Down from 19% (2015) to 15% (2025) of foreign-family purchases.
- **Top nationalities:** Mixed. Dutch, German, British in coastal and rural Lazio.

### 6.12 Veneto (excl. Venice)
- **Regional share:** ~12%, stable.
- **Top nationalities:** Germans, Austrians, Swiss (proximity to Lake Garda especially).
- **Hotspots:** Lake Garda (Bardolino, Sirmione, Lazise), Verona, the Prosecco hills.

### 6.13 Liguria
- **Top nationalities:** **French** (Imperia, Ventimiglia, Sanremo: border-hugging), **Germans** (Imperia, Riviera dei Fiori), Americans (Sanremo, Portofino).
- **Hotspots:** Portofino is a notable international luxury hotspot per Engel & Völkers.

### 6.14 Piedmont
- **Regional share:** ~6%.
- **Top nationalities:** Mixed European; Germans and Swiss strong; growing US interest in wine country (Langhe, Monferrato, Asti).
- **Hotspots:** Nizza Monferrato (Gate-Away top 10), Alba, Barolo area, Lago Maggiore (Verbano-Cusio-Ossola, particularly German-favored).

### 6.15 Trentino-Alto Adige / South Tyrol
- **Top nationalities:** Heavily Germanic. Germans and Austrians dominate; Swiss secondary.
- **Profile:** Geographic and linguistic proximity. Mountain second homes (Bolzano, Merano, Val Gardena, Madonna di Campiglio).

### 6.16 Friuli Venezia Giulia (excl. Trieste)
- **Top nationalities:** Austrians, Germans, Slovenians.

### 6.17 Calabria
- **Hotspots:** Scalea, Santa Maria del Cedro, Tropea.
- **Top nationalities:** Americans (rising, drawn by sub-€100K listings), Germans, Eastern Europeans (Polish, Czech).

### 6.18 Umbria
- **Regional share:** Collapsed from 10% (2015) to 2% (2025), the steepest decline of any Italian region.
- **Top nationalities:** British, Americans (Todi, Spoleto, Orvieto). Still attractive but losing share to Puglia/Sicily.

### 6.19 Amalfi Coast / Capri / Naples coastal
- **Top nationalities:** Americans (lead), British, Germans, Gulf states.
- **Profile:** Trophy luxury market; constrained supply; prices among the highest in Italy alongside Portofino and Costa Smeralda.

## 8. Key Trends (2023 to 2025)

1. **Foreign share keeps rising in absolute terms** despite some softness in inquiries: ~9.5% (2019) → 12% (2024) → ~14% (2025).
2. **South is overtaking Center.** Sicily +9pp, Sardinia +8pp, Puglia stable; Tuscany -9pp, Umbria -8pp, Lazio -4pp over a decade.
3. **Luxury foreign share is now decisive:** ~50 to 65% of prime transactions, up 44% YoY in dollar volume.
4. **Russian exit absorbed by Americans and Gulf buyers**, particularly in Sardinia/Costa Smeralda and Lake Como.
5. **Americans are the new top urban-luxury cohort**, particularly in Milan (26.56%), Florence, Rome, and Lake Como, replacing both UK (post-Brexit drag) and Russian flows.
6. **Mid-market growth** is driven by sub-€500K Adriatic, Calabrian, Sicilian, and Pugliese inventory, attracting British, Dutch, and emerging European buyers.

## 9. Methodological Notes And Open Questions

- **Closing data vs. inquiry data:** All numbers in this report distinguish between the two. Idealista and Gate-Away report inquiries/clicks; Scenari Immobiliari and Tecnocasa report closings. The two diverge significantly.
- **Definition of "foreign":** Most sources use buyer's country of citizenship, not residency. A US citizen with Italian residency may or may not be counted as "foreign" depending on dataset.
- **Luxury versus mass-market:** Luxury foreign-buyer percentages are not comparable to the national 14% figure; they are segment-specific.
- **Open questions worth deeper research if needed:**
  - Exact nationality breakdown for Naples, Bologna, Turin, Genoa, Palermo (data is sparse).
  - Cash-vs-mortgage split by nationality.
  - Buyer purpose split (primary residence vs. second home vs. investment) by city.
  - Visa-driven flows (Italy investor visa, "elective residency" visa) by nationality.
