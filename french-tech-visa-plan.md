## 1. Visa Track

**Primary: Passeport Talent – "French Tech Visa for Founders"** (Projet Économique Innovant)
- 4-year renewable residence permit, family included (spouse+kids get "Passeport Talent Famille")
- Path to 10-year carte de résident after 4 years
- Core requirement: **endorsement letter from an approved French incubator/accelerator** + proof of ~€22K/year resources (SMIC)
- No minimum investment, no need for pre-existing French SAS at application time

**Fallback: Passeport Talent – Entrepreneur**
- Requires €30K+ personal investment + Master's-level degree (MBA qualifies)
- Founder track is preferred because it rewards the innovation thesis

## 4. Legal + Fiscal Structure

- **Rarus Health SAS (France)** = R&D subsidiary of existing parent (US Delaware or Chilean)
- SAS signs **intercompany R&D services agreement** with parent: "SAS performs R&D, parent pays SAS cost + 8–10% markup"
- SAS is therefore profitable on paper from day 1, even with zero external revenue
- Salary flows from SAS, not from parent (critical for CIR eligibility + French tax residency)

### Tax incentives (CIR + JEI stack)

- **CIR (Crédit Impôt Recherche)**: 30% tax credit on qualified R&D spend, **cash-refundable** under JEI status
  - Includes **forfait de fonctionnement**: +43% on top of R&D salary base automatically
  - Example: €80K salary → €80K × 1.43 × 30% ≈ **€34K/year CIR**
- **JEI (Jeune Entreprise Innovante)**: employer social charge exemption (~42% of gross) on R&D staff for 8 years
  - Example: €80K salary → ~€30K/year employer charges exempted
- **Combined**: ~€60K/year relief on an €80K founder salary. SAS effectively pays ~€50K net to employ founder-engineer.

### Non-dilutive funding pipeline

- **Bpifrance French Tech Seed**: €90K non-dilutive, R&D milestones
- **i-Lab / i-Nov concours**: up to €600K non-dilutive, deep-tech R&D
- **Horizon Europe EIC Accelerator**: €2.5M grant + up to €15M equity (rare disease + AI is a current call priority)

## 5. Founder-as-R&D-Staff (Andrey specifically)

Founder engineering time fully qualifies as R&D staff for CIR/JEI purposes.

- Classification: **"assimilé ingénieur"** via MBA + 25+ years building tech (ex-Amazon) — no debate
- Title in SAS: *Président & Directeur Technique* (not pure "dirigeant"; dirigeant-only time is harder to claim)
- Salary **must** flow from French SAS, not US parent

### Audit-proofing requirements

1. **Weekly time log** separating R&D hours from admin/sales/investor hours (spreadsheet is fine)
2. **Annual technical dossier (état de l'art)**: 10–20 pages describing scientific/technical uncertainty, state of the art, experiments, results. Topics for Rarus:
   - WhatsApp-native clinical data extraction
   - Rare disease co-pilot RAG
   - Patient narrative generation
   - GDPR-compliant LLM pipelines
   - Keep ArXiv references
3. **Git commits, Jira, notebooks, design docs** preserved as audit evidence

Budget: 3 days/year for dossier writing. Skipping this is how founders get burned — CIR clawback + 40–80% penalty + interest.

## 6. Hiring Commitment — What's Actually Required

The visa dossier asks for a forward-looking **prévisionnel** (headcount plan). It is **not** legally binding employment contract. Residence permit is issued on project credibility at application time.

### Where hiring plan actually matters

1. **Year-4 renewal**: préfecture checks if SAS is active, filing accounts, shows economic substance. 1–2 hires + ongoing R&D is sufficient; 0 employees after 4 years triggers scrutiny.
2. **JEI/CIR audits** (the real risk): DGFiP audits up to 3 years back. Must have actual R&D activity with qualified staff. **Founder counts as qualified staff.**
3. **Endorser relationship**: missing stated hires damages reputation in small ecosystem.
4. **Bpifrance grant contracts**: have milestones. Clawback possible if missed.

### Sizing approach

- State numbers achievable even in a bad scenario
- Tie hiring to funded milestones: "post-seed round, X engineers within 12 months of closing"
- Hire person #1 within 6–9 months — the hinge for JEI eligibility, renewal story, endorser trust

### Committed hiring plan (for dossier)

> **Phase 1 (months 0–12):** Founder (CTO, ex-Amazon, 25+ years) relocates to France and conducts R&D full-time on the AI co-pilot and clinical data platform. SAS claims CIR on founder R&D time. JEI status granted.
>
> **Phase 2 (months 12–24, contingent on closing $1.2M pre-seed):** Hire 1–2 additional R&D engineers in France.
>
> **Phase 3 (months 24–36, post-seed):** Scale team to 3–5 FTEs; apply for i-Lab and Horizon Europe EIC Accelerator.

## 8. Risks and Mitigations

- **Residence obligation**: Passeport Talent holders must primarily live in France (>6 months/year). Plan US↔France rhythm accordingly.
- **Existing Chilean/US structure**: coordinate with tax lawyer on transfer pricing between Rarus SAS and existing entity to preserve CIR eligibility.
- **Founder dependency**: consulates scrutinize "is this person essential to the project." CTO role + IP listed in Andrey's name help — document before filing.
- **"Why France and not Portugal/Spain/Ireland?"**: answer = CIR + JEI + PariSanté + Inserm rare disease research density + EURORDIS HQ. No other EU country stacks all four.
- **Salary routing**: if US parent pays while physically in France → CIR ineligible + French tax residency complications. SAS must be the paying entity from day 1.
